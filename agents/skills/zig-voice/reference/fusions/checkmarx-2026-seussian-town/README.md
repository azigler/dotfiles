# Seussian Town — zig × 1990s children-book illustration × Seussian whimsy

A self-developed world-building aesthetic, not a co-branded fusion. Built
for Andrew's Checkmarx 2026 Agentic AppSec Summit talk ("Trust the
Agent. Verify the Code.") in May 2026, then extracted here as a
reusable bestiary + atlas + register the next talk or post can remix.

Lives here in `fusions/`, not in canonical zig references, because it
carries a specific WORLD (the town, the species, the flora) that
should not bleed into every future zig generation. Pull this folder
deliberately when you want this world.

## What this is

A tiny whimsical town inhabited by two cohesive species, set in a
rolling green-and-yellow landscape under a cobalt-and-marigold sky.
Rendered in 1990s children-book illustration with Seussian
architectural whimsy and the zig-coded bold-primary palette.

The talk's narrative use was "central plaza subject swaps per slide
to symbolize a control point" — but the world itself works for any
talk / post / asset that wants warm primary-color whimsy with a
recognizable recurring cast.

## When to reach for this register

Reach for it when:
- A long-form deck or post-series needs a recurring world the
  audience can re-recognize across many frames
- You want hand-painted children-book-illustration warmth (not 3D
  clay, not photorealism)
- You want bold-primary saturation, not pastels
- A character-driven scene would carry the post better than an
  abstract clay-pop subject

Don't reach for it when:
- The post is product-screenshot / dashboard / chart — keep canonical
  zig 3D clay or use a different register
- The audience needs a serious / enterprise / brand-formal tone
- The subject matter is dark, threatening, or adversarial — this
  world is friendly by construction

## The Atlas — the town

The canonical recurring environment. Same town across every frame;
only what happens inside it changes.

### Spatial structure

- A cobblestone plaza at the center, ringed by 6–10 huddled buildings
- The cobblestone street curves into the plaza from the foreground
- Buildings lean slightly into each other, asymmetric
- Rolling green-and-yellow hills behind the town
- Sky above with marigold sun-glow at the horizon

### Architecture

- Scalloped tile roofs in bold primary colors — sunflower-yellow,
  kelly-green, fire-engine-red (one color per roof, varied across
  buildings)
- Walls in warm cream with deep paneling and small arched / round
  windows
- Scalloped striped shop awnings (red/white, blue/white,
  yellow/white)
- Oversized rounded-top wooden doors
- Leaning brick chimneys with curlicue spiral wisps of bright
  marigold smoke rising from each one
- Slight asymmetric leaning gives each building personality

### Time of day variants (deck practice)

- **Afternoon** (default): cobalt sky, marigold sun-glow at horizon,
  soft light from upper-left
- **Sundown**: marigold-dominant in lower two-thirds of sky, deeper
  cobalt at top, long warm shadows, smoke from chimneys reads more
  orange
- **Dawn / sunrise**: warm orange behind distant hills, mist curling
  in the foreground, town tiny and barely lit in the distance
- **Festival night**: deep cobalt sky, glowing paper lanterns strung
  between buildings, curlicue fireworks against the dark, cobblestone
  reflecting lantern-light
- **Snow morning**: snowy scalloped rooftops, thick curlicue smoke
  plumes, pale cobalt-gray sky, cool palette dominant with bright
  primary accents on doors and clothing

## The Bestiary

Two cohesive species inhabit the town. ALL members of each species
are drawn identically except for clothing color. Never mix or hybridize
them. Never introduce a third species.

### Curl-snouts — the townspeople

Bipedal villagers, the only inhabitants of the plaza.

- Round pear-shaped body
- Stubby legs, short rounded arms
- A long snout curling upward at the tip in a spiral — like a tiny
  trunk ending in a curl. The curl is signature; without it they read
  as a different species.
- Two round button eyes
- Flat cap with a single tall feather sticking up
- About 1/8 the height of the buildings
- Solid-color one-piece clothing in primary palette (red, blue,
  yellow, green) — same garment shape across all curl-snouts; only
  color varies

**Child variant**: smaller-scale curl-snouts — about half the height
of adult curl-snouts, slightly rounder bodies, oversized caps, shorter
snouts that curl up in tiny spirals. Introduced for plaza-play /
walking-home-with-parent scenes.

**What they DO** in scenes: walk, carry parcels, reach for things,
tend gardens, operate machinery, talk in animated gestures, light
lanterns, push carts, play circle games (children), shovel snow,
tether tuft-tails to market stalls. They are ACTING, not standing.

### Tuft-tail grazers — the hill wildlife

Quadrupedal grazers, visible only on the hills behind the town. Never
enter the plaza (one explicit exception: scene where a single tuft-
tail peers from just outside the town gate).

- Small fuzzy round body
- Long curving neck
- Two large round button eyes
- Tiny rounded ears
- A single tufted feather on top of the head
- Four spindly stick-legs
- Long tail ending in a fluffy pom-pom

Distinctly different from curl-snouts: quadrupedal, fuzzy-bodied,
long-necked, never bipedal, never wear clothing, never in the plaza.

**What they DO**: graze, cluster in small groups at dawn, watch
silently from hillsides, mist curling around their legs, peer from
outside the gate (in the one explicit scene), accompany a curl-snout
traveler on the road (in another).

## The Monument Flora

A catalog of 5 silhouette types. Place 2–5 across the hills of any
scene, deliberately spaced — landmarks, not a forest. Vast empty hill
between each one.

1. **Pom-pom trunk** — tall impossibly slender curving trunk with a
   single pom-pom tuft at the top
2. **Curlicue cactus** — a lone spiraling cactus twisting upward
3. **Lollipop tree** — leaning, with a perfectly round canopy on an
   absurdly thin stem
4. **Striped-bulb pillar** — pillar-shaped plant crowned with a
   striped bulb
5. **Mushroom pair** — two mushroom-like silhouettes standing side
   by side

In autumn-season variants, the pom-poms turn burnt orange, the
lollipop canopy turns mustard-gold, fallen curlicue leaves drift.

## Palette — bold primaries, no pastels

The locked palette. Don't drift into pastels (we did this once in v2
of the smoke and the user pushed back hard).

- **Cobalt-blue** — primary sky color, candy-stripe accent
- **Marigold-orange** — primary sun-glow color, candy-stripe accent,
  chimney smoke
- **Sunflower-yellow** — roof color, hill highlights, clothing
- **Kelly-green** — roof color, hill base, clothing
- **Fire-engine-red** — roof color, awning stripes, clothing
- **Cream** — wall color, highlights

Hexes are approximate — the model interprets the names. If you need
exact, extract from the canonical reference images in `images/`.

## Aesthetic register

### Default (the hero batch) — bold + cartoony

- 1990s children-book illustration
- Hand-painted gouache with slight grain
- Confident bold outlines on shapes
- Flat shaded fills with painterly highlights
- Mood: warm, charming, retrofuturist edu-cartoon

### Painterly variant (the life batch + tall-v2 batch) — looser + cinematic

- Same palette + same world + same species
- BUT: visible loose brushwork, looser edges, less-clean outlines
- Atmospheric depth (haze, blurred backgrounds between subjects)
- Cinematic angles: low ground-level, worm's-eye, close to subjects,
  dynamic asymmetric composition
- Strong directional light per scene
- Mood: storybook-illustration depth instead of flat cartoon

**Both variants are valid.** Pick the painterly variant when you want
the scene to feel ALIVE (characters doing things, story unfolding);
pick the default when you want a clean hero subject prominently
posed.

## Hard rules (every generation)

- **NO real people or human-like figures** — only the two whimsical
  species defined here. The model loves to substitute humans when the
  prompt budget is too thin (it happened twice in this project's
  history).
- **NO text, letters, or numerals visible in image** — not on signs,
  scrolls, tablets, ledgers, clock faces (the clock has hands and
  hour marks — those are graphical, not numerals).
- **NO trademarked likenesses** — no Seuss-named species, no
  Truffula trees, no Sneetches, no Lorax. Invent fresh whimsical
  forms in that spirit.
- **NO white painterly vignette border / feathered edge / matting
  effect** — image must fill the full frame edge-to-edge. The
  painterly variant tempts the model to "feather" outward; explicitly
  forbid it.
- **Cohesive species within themselves** — all curl-snouts drawn
  identically, all tuft-tails drawn identically. Only clothing color
  varies for curl-snouts.

## The journey — how we arrived

A compressed log of the iterations that produced this register, so
future-you can either retrace or skip-to-the-end.

### Smokes (3 iterations on 2026-05-26)

- **v1**: clock tower + town. No flora, no creatures. Established
  basic Seussian architecture in zig palette.
- **v2**: added sparse monument flora (5 silhouette types).
  Established the "monuments not forest" rule.
- **v3**: bumped pastels → BOLD primaries; added the 2 species
  (curl-snouts + tuft-tails); locked clock tower to 2 clock faces.
  This is the locked register.

### Hero batches (default register)

- Wide hero (9 images, 16:9) — one per narrative beat in the talk's
  control-point spine
- Tall hero v1 (9 images, 9:16) — portrait recomposition of same 9
  subjects, to fit Gamma's default tall image slots

### Painterly batch (the breakthrough)

- Life vignettes (9 images, 16:9) — cinematic painterly scenes of the
  species LIVING in the town across the year (spring market morning,
  summer festival night, autumn harvest cart, winter snow, cafe
  closeup, worm's-eye clock tower, traveler at gate, children
  playing, tuft-tail herd at dawn)
- This batch broke open the painterly register. The user fed back
  "very, very good. I used all of them" and asked for the tall batch
  to be redone in the same register.

### Tall hero v2 (painterly redo)

- 9 portraits at 9:16, same subjects as tall-v1, redone in the
  painterly register with characters ACTING around each hero
  subject. Introduced the curl-snout child variant (in the sundown
  scene; first seen in the life batch's L08 children playing).

### Iteration totals

- 4 batches, 33+ generations, ~$3.30 nano-banana cost
- Plus 3 Gamma deck iterations along the way (one of which failed —
  see `~/cfp/talk-checkmarx-summit-2026/.beads/issues.jsonl` bead
  ej8.7 for the 5-reason diagnosis of why Gamma's per-slide
  imagery drifted off canonical)

## How to remix / extend this world

The intent is that future talks or posts can extend the bestiary,
the atlas, or the seasonal/scene variants without re-developing the
register from scratch.

### Easy remixes (low risk)

- **New scene in the same town**: a curl-snout opening a new shop, a
  curl-snout repairing a clock, two curl-snouts arguing in the
  plaza. Use the canonical shared block; specify the scene at the
  end.
- **New seasonal moment**: rainy day, eclipse, town fair, harvest
  moon. Add weather/light specification to the prompt; keep
  everything else canonical.
- **New monument flora variant**: a single new silhouette type can
  join the catalog (e.g., a candy-striped windmill, a tall vase of
  giant feathers). Keep it sparse.
- **New per-slide hero subject**: anything that fits in the plaza —
  a bandstand, a fountain, a town crier's bell, a giant gear.

### Higher-risk extensions (test before committing)

- **New species in the town**: don't. The bipedal cohesion was hard-
  won; adding a third species drifts the world.
- **Indoor scenes**: the world has always been outdoor + plaza. An
  indoor scene (a shop interior, a curl-snout's home) is possible
  but the canonical block would need a parallel "indoor block."
- **Architectural divergence**: a castle, a lighthouse, a covered
  bridge. Possible but think about whether it sits inside or beside
  the town.

### How to fire

The canonical generation pipeline:

1. Use the `~/.claude/skills/openrouter/openrouter-image.sh` helper
2. Pass `--aspect 16:9` (or `9:16` for portrait) and `--size 2K`
3. Attach the 10 zig-coded canonical anchors as `--ref` inputs
   (`~/.claude/skills/zig-voice/reference/*.{webp,png}`,
   non-recursive)
4. Prompt = the canonical shared environment block (below) + a
   per-image scene description (specific subject + action)
5. Don't attach images in `images/` here as `--ref` inputs — they're
   for human reference, not API anchors (the heavy refs trigger
   payload-size 502s in parallel — see ej8.10 lesson)

### Reusable canonical shared environment block

Paste this verbatim as the top of any new prompt for this world:

```
CANONICAL TOWN (Seussian-town fusion). A tiny whimsical town. Slightly
low ground-level camera angle into a central cobblestone plaza, with
a curving cobblestone street emerging from the bottom-foreground. 6-10
small huddled buildings ringing the plaza, leaning slightly. Each
building has a scalloped tile roof in a bold primary color (sunflower-
yellow, kelly-green, or fire-engine-red), walls in warm cream with
deep paneling, small arched windows, a scalloped striped shop awning
(red/white, blue/white, or yellow/white), an oversized rounded-top
wooden door, a leaning brick chimney with curlicue spiral wisps of
bright marigold smoke. Rolling grass-green and sunflower-yellow hills
behind. Cobalt-blue sky with marigold sun-glow at the horizon.
Generous upper-third negative space.

CANONICAL TOWNSPEOPLE — THE CURL-SNOUTS: 3-5 in any plaza scene,
ALL drawn identically (only clothing color varies). Pear-shaped round
body, stubby legs, short rounded arms, long snout curling upward at
the tip in a spiral, two round button eyes, flat cap with a single
tall feather sticking up. About 1/8 the height of the buildings.
Solid-color one-piece clothing in primary palette (red, blue, yellow,
green). Engaged in believable activity — NOT standing around.

CANONICAL HILL WILDLIFE — THE TUFT-TAIL GRAZERS: 1-3 on the hills,
ALL drawn identically. Small fuzzy round body, long curving neck, two
large round button eyes, tiny rounded ears, single tufted feather on
top of head, four spindly stick-legs, long tail ending in a fluffy
pom-pom. Never in the plaza.

CANONICAL MONUMENT FLORA: 2-5 silhouettes from the catalog sparsely
placed on hills (NOT a forest): pom-pom trunk, curlicue cactus,
lollipop tree, striped-bulb pillar, mushroom pair.

AESTHETIC: 1990s children-book illustration. Hand-painted gouache.
Bold primary palette only — NO pastels. Confident outlines, painterly
highlights.

HARD RULES: NO real people / human-like figures. NO text, letters,
or numerals visible in image. NO trademarked likenesses. Image fills
the full frame edge-to-edge — NO white painterly border, NO vignette,
NO feathered edges, NO matting.
```

For the painterly cinematic variant, add at the end:

```
PAINTERLY CINEMATIC REGISTER: looser visible brushwork than the
default. Atmospheric depth (haze / blur between foreground and
background planes). Cinematic low or close angle (specify per scene).
Strong directional light. Characters ACTING, not posed.
```

canonical anchors as API refs and treat these as catalog material
only.

  default register, bold primaries, cohesive species, monument flora.
  Use as visual reference for "what the world looks like at default."

  in animated cafe conversation. Reference for the painterly close-up
  variant + character expression.

  landscape composition + the herd-of-grazers possibility.
