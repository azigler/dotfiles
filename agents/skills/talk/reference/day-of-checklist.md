# Day-of checklist

Persistent reference. Lives as a typed `note` bead, **never closed** —
the checklist is the same shape for the next talk neighbor and the one
after that. Customize per-talk in the bead's `--notes` field.

---

## Night before

- [ ] **PDF export saved locally** to laptop (`gamma/exports/<conf>-final.pdf`)
- [ ] **PDF backup on USB stick** as a fallback (in case the laptop fails
      and a backup laptop is needed)
- [ ] **PDF uploaded to organizer's Google Form** (per the prep email)
- [ ] **Asana planning hub updated** — final gammaUrl + PDF link in a
      plaintext comment
- [ ] **Final dry-run runtime confirmed** ≤ runtime budget
- [ ] **Speaker's outfit set out** (presenter-comfortable, microphone-clip-
      friendly if a lavalier is used; the prep email or A/V notes will say)
- [ ] **Sleep schedule set** for the day-of arrival window

---

## Morning of

- [ ] **Breakfast** — protein + something familiar; not a brand-new
      restaurant pick
- [ ] **Hydration** — water bottle filled and packable
- [ ] **Outfit on** — comfortable, presentable, microphone-friendly
- [ ] **Bag check** (see "On-person checklist" below)

---

## On-person checklist (laptop bag)

| Item | Why | Backup? |
|---|---|---|
| **Laptop** with PDF + Gamma URL bookmarked | Primary delivery surface | — |
| **Laptop charger** | Battery anxiety is real | — |
| **HDMI adapter** (or USB-C / DisplayPort, per prep email) | Speaker podium connection | Backup adapter (organizer usually has one — confirm) |
| **Slide advancer (clicker)** | Frees the speaker from the laptop | Organizer usually has one — confirm |
| **PDF backup on USB stick** | Laptop failure recovery | — |
| **PDF emailed to self** | USB stick failure recovery (cloud-fetchable) | — |
| **Phone fully charged** | Track host text + organizer contact + photo of room | — |

## On-site arrival

Per the prep email — most conferences want speakers on-site **at
least 30 min prior** to the scheduled talk time.

- [ ] **Speaker registration check-in** — pick up speaker badge + any
      gift / swag / honorarium-related items
- [ ] **Walk to the track room** — note the route from the speaker
      registration to the room (often 5-10 min walk in big venues)
- [ ] **Arrive in the room ≥15 min prior** to the talk
- [ ] **Text the track host** — confirm you're in the room. The prep
      email has the host's mobile.

---

## In the room (T-15 min)

- [ ] **Find the A/V team** — usually at the back of the room. They
      handle mic accommodations.
- [ ] **Mic check** — lavalier vs. handheld vs. headset; confirm
      battery; do a sound check while the prior speaker is wrapping up
      OR during the change-over
- [ ] **Connect laptop to the podium HDMI** (or USB-C) — verify the
      slide projection works. **Don't trust the venue's pre-test;
      check it yourself.**
- [ ] **Verify the slide advancer** — confirm it advances forward and
      back, confirm the laser pointer if it has one
- [ ] **Open the PDF** (not the Gamma URL — local file is more
      reliable; presenting from web is a fallback only)
- [ ] **Set the speaker view** — laptop screen mirrors the projection
      OR shows the next-slide preview, depending on preference
- [ ] **Check the timer** — many venues have a "remaining time" timer
      visible to the speaker. Confirm where it is.
- [ ] **Check the lighting** — is the speaker lit? Is the audience
      lit? Some venues have a "lights-low audience, lights-on speaker"
      configuration that's different from the rehearsal-room setup.
- [ ] **Water on the podium** — if the venue provides it; if not, your
      bottle is on the podium

---

## T-5 min

- [ ] **Stand near the podium** so the host can introduce the speaker
      smoothly
- [ ] **Final hydration sip** — not a full chug; just enough to wet
      the throat
- [ ] **Phone on silent + airplane mode** — not just silent;
      airplane-mode prevents notifications from waking the laptop if
      it's mirrored
- [ ] **Laptop on "Do Not Disturb"** — no notifications during the
      talk

---

## During the talk

(The agent doesn't help here — this is delivery. The speaker has the
script + the deck + their preparation. The agent's job ends when the
day-of checklist is locked.)

A few mechanical notes the speaker might want to remember:

- **Look at the back of the room periodically** — keeps the eye-line
  high and the projection of voice strong
- **Hold bold-phrase slides for the marked beat** — the script's
  `*(Beat.)*` cues are non-negotiable for delivery rhythm
- **Don't rush the closer** — the closer is the most-photographed
  slide of the talk. The audience needs the full beat to absorb it.
- **End on "Thank you"** — the applause anchor. It signals the talk
  is over.

---

## Immediately after the talk

- [ ] **Walk to the Office Hours room** (or whatever the Q&A format
      is) — the prep email and the closer slide point the audience
      there
- [ ] **Take a photo of the room as the audience exits** — useful
      for `/zig-voice`'s post-talk content extraction
- [ ] **Text the track host** — confirm "talk done, walking to Office
      Hours"
- [ ] **Check the Asana hub** — leave a final plaintext comment ("talk
      delivered; recording will land at <YouTube URL TBD>")

---

## Post-Office Hours

- [ ] **Walk back to speaker lounge / hotel room** — adrenaline crash
      is real; budget 30+ min of low-energy time before any next
      commitment
- [ ] **Drop a closing typed `note` bead** — *"Handoff: talk
      delivered at <conf> on <date>. Next-stage skill: /zig-voice for
      content extraction."* Triggers the `/zig-voice` post-talk
      pickup.
- [ ] **DO NOT immediately start the recap LinkedIn post** — wait 24
      hours. Adrenaline-driven copy reads as adrenaline-driven copy.
      `/zig-voice` picks this up the day after.

## What goes wrong (and the fix)

| Failure mode | Mitigation | Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| Laptop fails | Backup laptop OR PDF on USB stick + venue laptop | Walk over with USB stick to A/V team; ask them to load the PDF on a venue laptop |
| HDMI doesn't work | Backup adapter | Borrow venue's adapter; many speaker podiums have one |
| Slide advancer fails | Manual click-through on laptop OR borrow venue's clicker | Switch to manual; preserve the runtime |
| Mic dies | Wireless mics have batteries — A/V team carries spares | Project to the back of the room and signal A/V; usually a 30-second swap |
| Audience is sparse | Same talk; same energy | The recording is the artifact for most viewers; deliver to the recording, not the room |
| Speaker forgets a line | Improvise; the slide is the cheat sheet | Slides are designed as cheat sheets — the speaker can read off the bold phrase if needed |
| Runtime drifting over | Cut a planned aside; tighten the closer | Pre-plan a "if I'm 60 sec over by Beat 4, drop the second example" branch |
| Q&A in Office Hours runs long | Set a hard exit time | The prep email usually has a track turnover schedule; honor it |

---

## The discipline

The day-of checklist is **not** about preparation — preparation
already happened, in the spec, the slide plan, the script, the dry
runs, the resource pack repo. The day-of is about **mechanical
reliability** — the laptop boots, the adapter fits, the mic works,
the timer is visible, the speaker is hydrated.

Mechanical reliability is what frees the speaker to focus on
delivery. That's the whole point of the checklist.
